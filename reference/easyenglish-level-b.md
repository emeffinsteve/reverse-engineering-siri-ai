# EasyEnglish Level B — Reference Document

EasyEnglish is a formally defined subset of standard English developed by
MissionAssist (formerly Wycliffe Associates UK). It uses ordinary English words
and standard English grammar, but restricts which words and grammatical
structures are permitted so that the result is far easier to read than ordinary
English. There are two vocabulary levels: **Level A** (~1,200 words, "very
easy") and **Level B** (~2,800 words, "easy").

This document records the Level B writing rules and whatever can be verified
about the Level B word list from publicly accessible sources.

---

## Writing rules (sourced)

Rules below are drawn from multiple pages on easyenglish.bible, from
missionassist.org.uk, and from the third-party summary at
learnthesewordsfirst.com. Where a number or phrase is quoted directly from a
source it is placed in quotation marks with the URL cited immediately after.

### Vocabulary restriction

- Level B is restricted to **"about 2800 words"** (also described as "around
  2,800 words").
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- Level B **"employs around 2,800 words and is directed at those with an
  intermediate level of proficiency in English."** It is described as **"about
  the same standard as the Cambridge First Certificate in English, which is a
  widely recognised standard throughout the world."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

- Level B is described as providing **"texts that exhibit a degree of style and
  sophistication"** compared with Level A — the larger vocabulary is the primary
  source of that additional expressiveness; the grammar rules are otherwise
  identical.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- Words outside the permitted Level B vocabulary that cannot be avoided are
  **marked with a \*star and defined at the end of most pages** (contrast Level
  A, where out-of-vocabulary words appear in italics).
  — <https://learnthesewordsfirst.com/about/what-can-you-read.html>

- MissionAssist maintains a private **lexicon** (and a developing thesaurus of
  permitted alternatives) used internally by trained writers to enforce
  consistent word choices.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- **Few abstract nouns** are permitted, because many first languages of target
  readers have relatively few nouns of this type.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Sentence length

- **Maximum 20 words per sentence.**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- **"Not more than two clauses per sentence"** (i.e. a maximum of two finite
  clauses). The Wycliffe Associates page phrases this as **"a maximum of two
  finite clauses per sentence is currently permitted."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- **Only one subordinate clause (one level of nesting) per sentence.** The
  communicator article states: **"EasyEnglish's solution is to allow only one
  subordinate clause (that is, only one instance of nesting) per sentence."** A
  clause embedded inside another subordinate clause is not permitted.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- **"Not more than two prepositional phrases per sentence."** Example given:
  "they ran to the shop in the village" (two prepositional phrases — permitted);
  adding "before sunset" would make three — not permitted.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### One idea per sentence / paragraph

- **"Only one topic per paragraph."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/press-releases/press-release-2013-12.htm>

- **Explicit connectors** between sentences are required so that logical flow is
  maintained even when sentences are short.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- Specific paragraph *word-count* limits are not stated in publicly available
  sources.

### Active voice

- **"No passive verbs"** (eewhatis.htm; press-release-2013-12.htm).
- The Wycliffe Associates page phrases it as **"EasyEnglish avoids almost all
  passives, because they are relatively complex forms for second-language
  readers"** — the "almost" acknowledges rare cases where an alternative would
  be too complex.
- The what-is page states: **"No passive constructions, except where any
  alternative is too complex."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

### Tense

- No specific tense restriction (e.g. "use present tense only") is stated in
  any publicly accessible EasyEnglish source. The style guide PDF has a "Tenses"
  section but that PDF is a scanned binary and could not be extracted.
  — Unverified; do not assume a rule exists.

### Pronouns

- **"No ambiguous pronouns"** — pronouns whose referent is unclear are
  eliminated or the noun is repeated.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

- Ambiguous genitive forms are also restricted: **"the genitive is restricted to
  forms that make the relationship between the terms unambiguous."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Idioms and metaphors

- **"No idioms."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/press-releases/press-release-2013-12.htm>

- **"No idiomatic expressions"** (what-is page).
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- Culturally-specific metaphors must be **"translated appropriately"** — the
  meaning is stated in plain words rather than carried over.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- The system makes **"implicit data explicit"** for readers from diverse
  cultural backgrounds.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Rhetorical questions

- **"No rhetorical questions"** (eewhatis.htm).
- The what-is page qualifies: **"No rhetorical questions, except where the
  implied answer is clear."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

### Split infinitives

- **"No split infinitives."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

### Technical and difficult words

- Any word not in the Level B vocabulary that cannot be avoided (proper nouns,
  Bible-specific terms, technical terms) must be:
  1. **Marked with a \*star** at first use, and
  2. **Defined in a word list at the end of the page/document**, with the
     definition itself written in Level B vocabulary.
  — <https://learnthesewordsfirst.com/about/what-can-you-read.html>

- Bible commentaries written at Level B include such a word list; the
  easyenglish.bible/bible-study/research.htm page explicitly notes: **"These
  are all written in Level B EasyEnglish (2800 word vocabulary) but do not
  comply fully with our simple grammar rules."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/bible-study/research.htm>

### Level B vs Level A: key differences

| Feature | Level A | Level B |
|---|---|---|
| Vocabulary size | ~1,200 words | ~2,800 words |
| Target audience | English learners (basic/social) | Intermediate second-language speakers |
| Proficiency benchmark | Basic working English | Cambridge First Certificate in English |
| Style | Minimal | **"A degree of style and sophistication"** |
| Out-of-vocabulary marker | Italics | \*Star + end-of-page definition |

Grammar rules (clauses, passives, idioms, etc.) appear to be **identical across
both levels**; the vocabulary expansion is the sole formal difference between
Level A and Level B as stated in publicly available sources.

### Summary table

| Rule | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Vocabulary size | ~2,800 words (Level B) |
| Words per sentence | Max 20 |
| Finite clauses per sentence | Max 2 |
| Subordinate clause nesting | Max 1 level |
| Prepositional phrases per sentence | Max 2 |
| Topics per paragraph | 1 |
| Passive voice | Forbidden ("almost all" — rare exceptions permitted) |
| Ambiguous pronouns | Forbidden |
| Ambiguous genitives | Forbidden |
| Idioms | Forbidden |
| Metaphors (culturally specific) | Replace with plain explanation |
| Rhetorical questions | Forbidden (or only where answer is obvious) |
| Split infinitives | Forbidden |
| Abstract nouns | Minimised |
| Out-of-vocabulary words | \*Star + define in end-of-page word list |
| Tense restriction | Not publicly specified |
| Direct vs. indirect speech | Not publicly specified |

## Level B word list

**The full Level B ~2,800-word list is NOT publicly available in extractable
text form.** MissionAssist holds the vocabulary in a proprietary lexicon and
computer-assisted writing tool used internally by trained EasyEnglish writers.
No HTML page or extractable-text document reproducing the complete list was
found after searching easyenglish.bible, missionassist.org.uk, and third-party
sources.

The style guide PDF at
<https://www.easyenglish.bible/wp2014/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/easyenglish-style-guide.pdf>
is a scanned binary image (not machine-readable text) and could not be
extracted.

### What is publicly documented about the word selection

- Source corpus: **top 3,000 words by frequency in the British National
  Corpus**, further curated to ~2,800 for Level B.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- Each selected word is permitted in its most common meaning(s). Level B allows
  a somewhat wider range of meanings per word than Level A.

- **Few abstract nouns** make it into Level B.

- Words are checked against the lexicon using **a dedicated computer program**
  (mentioned in multiple sources as part of the production workflow).
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

- A writers' manual, a lexicon, a biblical glossary, and a computer program have
  been produced to help create EasyEnglish materials to a consistent standard,
  but none of these are publicly downloadable.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

### Partial public proxy: the Easy English Bible Dictionary

The **Easy English Bible Dictionary** at <https://www.easyenglish.bible/dictionary/bible/>
explains **"the meaning of more than 1,000 difficult words that are in the
EasyEnglish Bible"** and is itself written in Level B vocabulary. Its headword
list is a **partial and biblical/theological subset** of the Level B vocabulary
— it documents words that go *beyond* ordinary Level B rather than the full
~2,800-word core list.

**This dictionary is NOT the Level B word list.** It skews heavily toward:
- Biblical proper nouns (Abraham, Assyria, Gethsemane, etc.)
- Religious and liturgical terms (atonement, baptism, covenant, etc.)
- Ancient Near Eastern objects and customs (ephod, cubit, sackcloth, etc.)

These are precisely the words most likely to be *outside* everyday Level B
vocabulary and therefore requiring definition.

The dictionary headwords extracted from the A–P portion of the page include
terms across approximately 100–170 entries per letter section (A through P
visible). The Q–Z portion also exists but was not fully retrievable in a
non-hallucinated form. The complete dictionary is estimated at ~1,000+ entries.

### Partial / proxy sources

No third-party site was found that reproduces the EasyEnglish Level B word list
in full. The **New General Service List (NGSL)** — a ~2,809-word frequency list
derived from the Cambridge English Corpus (2013, updated 2016 and 2023) — is
**not** the same as the EasyEnglish Level B list, though both target ~2,800
words.
— <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_General_Service_List>

**Do not treat any generic B1-CEFR or "2,000–3,000 most common English words"
list as equivalent to the EasyEnglish Level B list.** The EasyEnglish lexicon
was specifically curated for a Biblical/religious context and for readers from
diverse cultural backgrounds. It excludes common English words that carry heavy
cultural loading or multiple meanings, and it includes specialised biblical
vocabulary not present in frequency-based general lists.

To obtain the list, contact MissionAssist directly:
<https://missionassist.org.uk/volunteering/english-language/writing-in-easyenglish/>
or <ask@easyenglish.bible>
