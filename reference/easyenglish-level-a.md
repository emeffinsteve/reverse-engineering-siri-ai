# EasyEnglish Level A — Reference Document

EasyEnglish is a formally defined subset of standard English developed by
MissionAssist (formerly Wycliffe Associates UK). It uses ordinary English words
and standard English grammar, but restricts which words and grammatical
structures are permitted so that the result is far easier to read than ordinary
English. There are two vocabulary levels: **Level A** (~1,200 words, "very
easy") and **Level B** (~2,800 words, "easy").

This document records the Level A writing rules and whatever can be verified
about the Level A word list from publicly accessible sources.

---

## Writing rules (sourced)

Rules below are drawn from three pages on easyenglish.bible and from the
MissionAssist website. Where a number or phrase is quoted directly from a source
it is placed in quotation marks with the URL cited immediately after.

### Vocabulary restriction

- Level A is restricted to **"about 1200 words"** (also described as "around
  1,200 words").
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- The vocabulary derives from the top 3,000 words by frequency in the British
  National Corpus, then reduced further. The selection favours **"the most
  frequently used meaning (or meanings)"** of each word; at Level A each word
  is usually permitted only in one meaning.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- **Few abstract nouns** are permitted (e.g. *strength*, *anger*, *peace*),
  because many first languages of target readers have relatively few nouns of
  this type.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- Words outside the permitted vocabulary that must appear (e.g. biblical names
  or technical terms) are **written in italics** with a definition provided
  either as a hover/click gloss or in an A–Z word list at the end of the
  document.
  — <https://learnthesewordsfirst.com/about/what-can-you-read.html>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- MissionAssist maintains a private **lexicon** (and a developing thesaurus of
  permitted alternatives) to help writers check word choices consistently.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Sentence length

- **Maximum 20 words per sentence.**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- **"Not more than two clauses per sentence"** (i.e. a maximum of two finite
  clauses).
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- **Only one subordinate clause (one level of nesting) per sentence** — a
  clause embedded inside another subordinate clause is not permitted.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- **"Not more than two prepositional phrases per sentence."** Example given:
  "they ran to the shop in the village" (two prepositional phrases — permitted);
  adding "before sunset" would make three — not permitted.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### One idea per sentence / paragraph

- **"Only one topic per paragraph."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

- The writer identifies "idea units" in a complex source passage and
  **"arranges them in logical order,"** then reassembles them as a series of
  short, simple sentences that build the reader's knowledge step by step.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- **Explicit connectors** between sentences are required so that logical flow
  is maintained even when sentences are very short.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- Specific paragraph *word-count* limits are not stated in publicly available
  sources.

### Active voice

- **"No passive verbs"** (from eewhatis.htm).
- The Wycliffe Associates page phrases it as **"EasyEnglish avoids almost all
  passives"** — the "almost" acknowledges rare cases where an alternative would
  be too complex.
- The what-is page states: **"No passive constructions, except where any
  alternative is too complex."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

### Tense

- No specific tense restriction (e.g. "use present tense only") is stated in
  any publicly accessible EasyEnglish source. The style guide PDF
  (easyenglish-style-guide.pdf) has a "Tenses" section but that PDF is a
  scanned binary and could not be extracted.
  — Unverified; do not assume a rule exists.

### Pronouns

- **"No ambiguous pronouns"** — pronouns whose referent is unclear are
  eliminated or the noun is repeated.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

- Ambiguous genitive forms are also restricted.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Direct vs. indirect speech

- No specific rule about preferring direct over indirect speech (or vice versa)
  appears in publicly accessible sources. The style guide PDF has no extractable
  text. This is unverified.

### Idioms and metaphors

- **"No idioms"** — idiomatic expressions are forbidden.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

- **"No idiomatic expressions"** (phrased identically in what-is page).

- Culturally-specific metaphors must be **"translated appropriately"** — the
  meaning of the figure of speech is stated in plain words rather than
  carrying the metaphor over.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-us/articles/communicator.htm>

- The system makes **"implicit data explicit"** for readers from diverse
  cultural backgrounds (e.g. background knowledge that a source text assumes
  is stated outright).
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

### Rhetorical questions

- **"No rhetorical questions"** (eewhatis.htm).
- The what-is page qualifies this: **"No rhetorical questions, except where
  the implied answer is clear."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>

### Split infinitives

- **"No split infinitives."**
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

### Technical and difficult words

- Any word not in the Level A vocabulary that cannot be avoided (proper nouns,
  Bible-specific terms, technical terms) must be:
  1. **Defined in context** or explained in plain Level A words immediately
     after first use, and/or
  2. **Listed in an A–Z word list** at the end of the document (or in a
     separate glossary), each definition itself written in Level A vocabulary.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/what-is/>
  — <https://learnthesewordsfirst.com/about/what-can-you-read.html>

### Summary table

| Rule | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Vocabulary size | ~1,200 words (Level A) |
| Words per sentence | Max 20 |
| Finite clauses per sentence | Max 2 |
| Subordinate clause nesting | Max 1 level |
| Prepositional phrases per sentence | Max 2 |
| Topics per paragraph | 1 |
| Passive voice | Forbidden (near-absolute) |
| Ambiguous pronouns | Forbidden |
| Idioms | Forbidden |
| Metaphors (culturally specific) | Replace with plain explanation |
| Rhetorical questions | Forbidden (or only where answer is obvious) |
| Split infinitives | Forbidden |
| Abstract nouns | Minimised |
| Out-of-vocabulary words | Italicise + define in glossary/word list |
| Tense restriction | Not publicly specified |
| Direct vs. indirect speech | Not publicly specified |

## Level A word list

**The full Level A ~1,200-word list is NOT publicly available in extractable
text form.** MissionAssist holds the vocabulary in a proprietary lexicon and
computer-assisted writing tool used internally by trained EasyEnglish writers.
No HTML page or extractable-text document reproducing the complete list was
found after searching easyenglish.bible, missionassist.org.uk, and third-party
sources.

The style guide PDF at
<https://www.easyenglish.bible/wp2014/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/easyenglish-style-guide.pdf>
is a scanned binary image (not machine-readable text) and could not be
extracted.

### What is publicly documented about the word selection

- Source corpus: **top 3,000 words by frequency in the British National
  Corpus**, reduced to ~1,200 for Level A.
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/about-easyenglish/wycliffe-associates-easyenglish/>

- Each selected word is permitted in **one (or very few) of its most common
  meanings** only.

- **Few abstract nouns** make it into Level A.

- Words are checked against the lexicon using **a dedicated computer program**
  (mentioned in multiple sources as part of the production workflow).
  — <https://www.easyenglish.bible/eewhatis.htm>

### Partial / proxy sources

No third-party site was found that reproduces the EasyEnglish Level A word list
in full. The closest public proxy is the **New General Service List (NGSL)**
— a ~2,800-word frequency list derived from the British National Corpus
(Wikipedia: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_General_Service_List>) — but
this is Level B territory (EasyEnglish Level B is also ~2,800 words). The
Level A ~1,200-word list would be a strict subset.

**Do not treat any generic A1-CEFR or "1,000 most common English words" list
as equivalent to the EasyEnglish Level A list.** The EasyEnglish lexicon was
specifically curated for a Biblical/religious context and for readers from
diverse cultural backgrounds, and it excludes many common English words that
carry heavy cultural loading or multiple meanings.

To obtain the list, contact MissionAssist directly:
<https://missionassist.org.uk/volunteering/english-language/writing-in-easyenglish/>
or <ask@easyenglish.bible>.
