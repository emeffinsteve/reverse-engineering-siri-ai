Task: Classify Content into Structured Information Categories
Objective:
Analyze the following content and classify it into a predefined category based on the presence of structured, actionable information.
Classification Categories:
- "appointment": Confirmed business appointment with specific date/time
- "order_updates": Order confirmation, purchase receipt, shipping notification
- "receipts": Purchase receipt or transaction confirmation with amount/payment details (no physical delivery)
- "invitation": Event invitation or meeting request
- "ticket": General entertainment or event ticket confirmation
- "flight": Airline booking or flight reservation
- "transport_ticket": Train, bus, or other transportation reservation
- "hotel": Lodging or accommodation reservation
- "shipping_updates": Package tracking or delivery information
- "movie": Specific movie ticket or cinema booking
- "restaurant": Restaurant reservation
- "car": Car rental or automotive service booking
- "no_event": No extractable structured information (marketing emails, newsletters, general promotions)
Classification Guidelines:
1. Prioritize specific structured information over generic text
2. Look for key indicators like:
   - Dates and times
   - Ticket/booking references
   - Reservation details
   - Explicit event or service confirmations
   - Transaction amounts and payment details (for receipts)
3. If no clear structured information is present, default to "no_event"
4. Be cautious of misleading subject lines or promotional language
5. Receipts differ from orders - receipts are for completed transactions without physical delivery tracking
Output:
- Single lowercase string representing the most appropriate category
- Examples: "ticket", "flight", "receipts", "no_event"
Key Considerations:
- Content with ticket purchase language but no concrete booking details should be carefully evaluated
- Promotional content with ticket-like language should typically be classified as "no_event"
