**1. NICKNAMES**
Extract names/terms the user uses to directly address the recipient.

INCLUDE:
- Name variations (Mike for Michael, Lizzie for Elizabeth)
- Terms of endearment (honey, babe, love, sweetie)
- Casual address terms (dude, buddy, homie)

EXCLUDE:
- Greeting words (Hi, Hello, Hey) - these are salutations
- Names used by others to address the user
- Group references (team, all, everyone, home)
- Third-party references
- Names that appear in signatures or sign-offs
- Punctuation should be included only if it's part of how the user consistently addresses someone (e.g., "Haley:" if the colon is always used)


**2. SALUTATIONS**
Extract short greeting words and formal structures (without names) at the very beginning of a mail.
**CRITICAL DEFINITION**: Salutations are SHORT GREETING WORDS or FORMAL STRUCTURES (typically 1-3 words) used to address the recipient at the very beginning of an email. They are NOT conversation starters or opening substantive phrases.

INCLUDE:
- Formal structures (Dear, Greetings, To whom it may concern)
- Greeting words/phrases (Hi, Hello, Hey, Good morning)

EXCLUDE:
- Opening conversation starters (Hope you're well, How are you). These should belong to openings
- Reply-specific starters (Thanks for your email, Following up on). These should belong to openings
- Person names or nicknames within greetings: "Hi John", "Hey Karen" (Only extract "Hi", "Hey" - John and Karen belong to nicknames)
- Closing phrases or sign-offs: "Thanks", "Best regards" (these belong to sign-offs)
- Middle-of-email content or non-greeting phrases

- \u2713 Valid: "Dear" (1 word, formal structure)
- \u2717 Invalid: "Thanks for your email" (reply starter, this is an opening)
- \u2713 Valid: "Good morning" (2 words, greeting phrase)
- \u2717 Invalid: "I hope this email finds you well" (conversation starter, this is an opening)
